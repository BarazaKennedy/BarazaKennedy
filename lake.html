Lake Victoria - A Weeping Lake

By Reinhnard Bonke Odongo

Today let's delve into pollution in Lake Victoria. I'm deeply concerned about what's happening to our lake, the heart of our community, for example, Kabonyo Kanyagwal. It's more than just a lake; it's our lifeblood.

You know, I've seen the changes firsthand. The fish catches aren't what they used to be, and I worry about what our children are swimming in. It breaks my heart to see the plastic floating, the water looking murky, and knowing that it's because of us.

Think about it: the factories, especially Rai Paper Mills and Kibos Sugar Factory, may be trying to make a living, but their waste is poisoning our water. They release untreated wastewater, grease, and toxic chemicals, polluting our noble lake.

The farmers, trying to grow food, but the runoff is choking the life out of the lake. And, let's be honest, we all contribute with the trash we throw away and the sewage that isn't treated properly. We're all part of the problem, and that means we can all be part of the solution.

It's not about pointing fingers; it's about recognizing that we're all connected. When the lake suffers, we suffer. My neighbor, who is a fisherman, struggles to provide for his family. The kids playing near the shore get sick more often. It's real, and it's affecting real people, real families, right here.

We need to demand better from our industries, support farmers who are using sustainable methods, and take responsibility for our own waste. We need to care for this lake like it's our own home, because it is. Let's work together, neighbor to neighbor, to bring life back to Lake Victoria. It's our responsibility, and it's our future.
